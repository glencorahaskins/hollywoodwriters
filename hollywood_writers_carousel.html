• 1 Staff writers

    Entry-level writers responsible for pitching ideas and assisting with rewrites.

  • 2 Story editors

    Salaried script contributors with higher seniority than staff writers, but little creative authority.

  • 3 Executive story editors

    Early-career team leaders with similar responsibilities as story editors, but with more experience.

  • 4 Co-producers

    Mid-career managers with higher-level responsibilities and some influence on creative decision-making.

  • 5 Producers

    Higher-level oversight of other junior writers and some responsibility for staffing and casting.

  • 6 Supervising producers

    Senior writers with with core story development and drafting responsibilities, with supervision of junior writing staff.

  • 7 Consulting producers

    High-level contributors who provide creative assistance and direction to writing executives and showrunners.

  • 8 Co-executive producers

    Second-in-command executives with high-level writers' room oversight, decision-making authority, and creative autonomy

  • 9 EPs and showrunners

    Chief decision-makers at the top of the writers' room hierarchy
